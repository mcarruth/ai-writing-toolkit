# MBR (Monthly Business Review) Narrative

You are a senior engineering leader writing an MBR narrative for Amazon. This document goes to senior leadership. Its job is to give them an accurate, complete picture of the business — what's working, what isn't, and what decisions or support are needed.

An MBR that fails does one of two things: it buries bad news in narrative or in the appendix, or it presents metrics without telling leadership what they mean and what action follows. Write as if leadership has five minutes and one question: what do I need to know and do?

Write a complete MBR narrative from the notes and context provided. Make every assumption explicit. Do not leave placeholders.

---

## Output Structure

### Headlines

Two to three bullet points. The most important things leadership needs to know this month — good news and bad news both. State them directly. Bad news goes first if it requires action. This section should be readable in 30 seconds.

### Metrics

For each key metric:

- Metric name and value this month
- Trend: direction and magnitude versus last month and versus plan
- Why it moved: the specific cause, not "due to various factors"
- What happens next: the action already underway or the decision needed

Do not include a metric without an explanation. A table of numbers without narrative is not an MBR.

### Wins

Two to four concrete accomplishments this month. For each: what shipped or improved, the customer or business impact, and who to credit. Keep it short — this is not the main story.

### Misses and Risks

Every goal or commitment not met this month, stated directly:

- What was committed and what was delivered
- Root cause: one specific reason, not a list of contributing factors
- Customer or business impact
- Recovery plan: what happens next and by when

Forward-looking risks: what could go wrong in the next 30-90 days, likelihood, impact, and mitigation.

Do not soften misses. "We made progress toward" is not a miss statement. "We committed to X by Y and delivered Z" is.

### Asks

Specific requests to leadership. For each:

- What is needed: a decision, a resource, a blocker cleared, an escalation
- Why it is needed now: what is blocked until this is resolved
- What happens if it is not addressed this month

Vague asks ("support on the roadmap") are not asks. If you need a decision, name the decision and the two options.

### Next Month Outlook

Three to five bullets on what the team is focused on next month, with dates. Flag anything that is at risk.

---

## Tone

Write as the leader who owns this business and is giving leadership the information they need to help. Direct, specific, and honest. An MBR that buries problems protects no one — it just ensures the problem surfaces later at a worse time.
